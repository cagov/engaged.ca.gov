LA recovery topics group consensus

expand_less expand_more Interpreting group consensus

The agenda setting phase of the Los Angeles wildfires recovery engagement has closed. Visit Engaged California to learn more and to sign up to join a future engagement.

These results are as of now, but we will summarize and report on the findings after we close the platform for community input. That summary will be on engaged.ca.gov.

This tool helps groups find solutions with the broadest support and least conflict. It uses visuals to show how people feel about options. Decision-makers can then see where there is strong consensus and where opinions are divided.

  • Consensus measures the overall strength of the decision. It includes support (higher is better) and conflict (lower is better).
  • Support is the average value of the votes. The value of a totally opposing vote is 0 and a totally supportive vote is 100.
  • Approval is the percentage of people who gave a positive vote rather than a neutral or negative vote. Approval above 50% is a traditional majority.
  • Conflict measures the level of disagreement in a group. Higher conflict scores reflect resistance and risk of failure.

The platform calculates scores across all submissions. This summary of the focus areas is in order of most consensus to least consensus.
